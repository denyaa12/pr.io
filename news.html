PR Industry News

1. Growing Demand for Digital PR

PR agencies are expanding digital services, including influencer partnerships, social media promotion (TikTok, Telegram), and online reputation management (ORM). Short-term PR campaigns for specific products are especially popular in 2024-2025.

2. AI in PR: ChatGPT and Neural Networks

Many agencies now use AI tools for media analysis, press release drafting, and journalist outreach. Tools like DeepSeek help predict media trends and optimize PR strategies.

3. Declining Trust in Media Boosts PR Importance

With rising misinformation, brands increasingly rely on PR agencies to shape narratives through expert articles, podcasts, and corporate blogs.

4. New PR Formats

  • Metaverse PR (brand promotions in virtual worlds),
  • Gamified PR (campaigns in Roblox or Web3 projects),
  • Stealth PR (undercover promotion via micro-influencers).

5. Mergers and Acquisitions

Major holding companies (e.g., WPP, Publicis) are acquiring niche PR agencies specializing in ESG, fintech, and biotech.

6. Tighter Regulations

The EU and US are cracking down on hidden ads and native integrations, forcing agencies to adapt their strategies.

7. Russian PR Market Shifts

Post-2022, agencies pivoted to Asia/Middle East markets, government/state-owned clients, and crisis communications.
